01 — The Core Problem
1,956 posts. 72,000 followers. 15 years of work. All of it sitting on a platform you don't own, controlled by an algorithm that decides who sees it and when.

Instagram is excellent for discovery. It is terrible for conversion, retention, and revenue. When Randal posts a finished painting, Meta decides whether 3% or 30% of his audience sees it. No email list backup. No SEO value. No long-term asset being built.

The strategy is not to leave Instagram. It's to stop treating it as the destination and start using it as the funnel.

06 — Caption Framework

The 4-part formula.
Works for any post format.

Most artist captions are either pure description ("new painting, link in bio") or wall-of-text philosophy. Neither converts. This structure does.

1
The Hook
First line. Stops the scroll. Named emotion, unexpected observation, or a direct question. Never "New work:" or "Excited to share."
2
The Story
2–4 sentences. The idea behind the piece, the moment it came from, or the question it's asking. Not a description of what's in the image.
3
The Bridge
Connect the painting to the person reading. "If you've felt this—" or "This one's for anyone who—" is the move from art to relationship.
4
The CTA
One clear next step. Never two. Drop link, email signup, Patreon, or simply "Let me know what you feel." Rotate based on what's active.
