Growth Strategy & Revenue Architecture

$60K at home is the floor,
not the ceiling.

A sequenced plan for the space, the team, and the income that (eventually) works without you.

01 — Revenue Streams

Six streams. In order of activation.

  • 01
    1:1 Personal Training (Active)35 sessions/week at $35–$70/session. The current engine. Ceiling is ~40 sessions before burnout. Expansion requires the space and hires below.
  • 02
    Referral Program (Near-term)Formalize the existing word-of-mouth network. A simple incentive ($20 credit, a session discount) turns passive referrers into active ones. Cost: near zero. Return: consistent.
  • 03
    Group Clinics / Workshops (Near-term)2–4 person workshops. Low overhead, premium pricing, reaches clients who can't afford 1:1. This is a gateway to the ongoing relationship.
  • 04
    Physical Studio — Expanded Capacity (6–18 months)2,000 sq ft, 2–3 trainers. Infrastructure cost ~$3,000–$5,000/mo. At 70–100 sessions/week across the team, revenue doubles to triple. Requires the business plan, the hire system, and the onboarding docs first.
  • 05
    Train-the-Trainer Program (12–24 months)Your real edge: teaching trainers how to work with aging bodies safely. A 1-day clinic, a certification track, or a consulting arrangement with other studios — each of these compounds.
  • 06
    Speaking + Thought Leadership (Ongoing)Talks to corporations, medical practices, senior communities. Low effort once the positioning is documented — and a direct pipeline to group contracts and referral relationships.
02 — The Business Plan Priority

The bank needs paper.
Let's write the paper.

The most immediate deliverable isn't a website (though we'll make that legit too.) It's a business plan that can open a line of credit or support a lease. Documented revenue, projected capacity, staffing model, clear narrative.

  • 01
    Revenue documentation.Organize Venmo / bank records into a clean 12-month P&L. Self-employed income is real — it just needs to be legible.
  • 02
    Projected income model.Conservative, realistic, and optimistic scenarios for the expanded space. Numbers a lender can evaluate.
  • 03
    Staffing + overhead model.What 2 trainers cost, what they generate, and what the break-even looks like on a $3,500–$5,000/mo lease.
  • 04
    Executive summary.A one-pager that tells the story: who you are, what you've built, and why the expansion is low-risk.
