Brand Voice Guide

The brand should sound like she actually talks.

This guide was built from three sources: the March 2026 meeting transcript, the live meganrising.com site, and the complete Notion sitemap & copy doc. The goal is to close the gap between Megan in conversation — warm, irreverent, direct — and the website copy, which sometimes reads more polished than she actually sounds.

Five words that define how Megan sounds

Earthy This isn't a metaphor. She lives on 27 acres in Floyd, VA — spring water, creek bathing, garden fencing. The land is the product. Copy should feel like it has soil under its fingernails.
Feral Not wild in a Disney-princess way. Wild like she moved onto raw land with a camper and a vision. The women she calls to are ready to shed the performance — not polish it.
Cosmic Stars, charts, transits, natal maps. But always embodied — felt in the body, not just understood in the head. "The entire universe is inside of you and your natal chart is a map of that universe."
Pleasure-positive Pole dancing at Beltane. Flower crowns at golden hour. Indian dinner by firelight. "Let's put the glamour in glamping." The sacred and the sensual are not opposites here — they're the same thing.
Direct No Instagram wellness. No spiritual bypassing. "A little crunchy, a lot glam, all truth." When she means something, she says it. The copy earns every word or cuts it.

How she builds a sentence

Short declarative → opens into body

Hits you with a short punch, then the explanation flows out. Often with an em dash. The rhythm is conversational, not stiff. She earns the longer sentence by landing the short one first.

"No Instagram wellness. No spiritual bypassing. Just earth, fire, stars, and the freedom to be exactly who you are."

Both/and framing

"A little crunchy, a lot glam." "Sweetness, and a little bit of spice." She doesn't flatten complexity — contradiction is the brand. The tension between opposites is where she lives.

"Bring your best energy, your wildest self — the version of you that's ready to be fully seen."

"Remember" as verb and homecoming

Women come to The Meadow to remember who they are. The word is a recurring motion in her copy. It signals reclamation without blame — you didn't lose yourself, you just forgot.

"Where women gather to shed the performance and remember their truth."

Cosmic + body, always together

She never does just stars, and she never does just body. The translation always goes both directions: the chart into the lived experience, the body back into the cosmos. Separate them and it's not her.

"Feel it in your bones — not just understand in your head. That's what embodied astrology means."

Invitation, not command

"Come ready to work, play, and build something beautiful together." "Bring your best energy." She invites — she doesn't push. Consent is the operating system of everything she does, and the copy reflects that.

"If this offends you, reach out to discuss — or this may not be an aligned match."

Place as a character

"27 acres in the Blue Ridge Mountains." "Floyd, VA." "The creek." "The yurt." The geography is grounding and specific — it's never generic "nature" or "land." The Meadow is a named, living thing.

"Nestled on 27 acres of quiet meadowland in Floyd, VA — return to what's wild and natural."

The language map

✓ Say this

  • "Remember" — the brand's core motion; women come to reclaim what they forgot
  • "Wild women / feral women" — her exact audience language; own it
  • "The Meadow" — always a proper noun; the land is a named character
  • "Embodied" — grounded in what it means: felt in the body, not just understood in the head
  • "Shed the performance" — the transformation mechanism, say it this way
  • "Cosmic blueprint" — her signature astrology frame; use it consistently
  • "Wheel of the year / seasonal" — Beltane, solstice, equinox are the rhythm
  • "Both/and" contrast pairs — "a little crunchy, a lot glam"; "sweetness, and a little spice"
  • Em dashes — she thinks in dashes; the copy should too
  • "Earth, fire, stars" — the sensory brand trinity

× Avoid this

  • "Manifest / manifestation" — too Instagram, no texture for her brand
  • "Sacred container / hold space" — she does this, but the phrases are done
  • "Healing journey" — say what the journey actually is; specificity is everything
  • "Women's empowerment" — not her lane, too polished and institutional
  • "Your highest / best self" — generic, not her voice
  • "Transform your life" — too vague, no felt sense
  • "Ancient wisdom" — say which tradition, which practice specifically
  • "It's time to..." — every wellness brand on Instagram uses this
  • "Spiritual journey" — cliché; say what the path actually looks like
  • Distance-creating polish — this brand lives close; the copy should too
