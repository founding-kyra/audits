Phase 2 · Structural Context

How the business actually moves

  • Discovery

    Instagram / word of mouth. No SEO inbound yet.

  • Lead Capture

    No lead magnet. Contact form only. Instagram follows are the list.

  • Entry Offer

    30-min reading or Patreon — neither clearly "start here."

  • Conversion

    Retreat registration via Tally forms. Until live, visitors DM or bounce.

  • Recurring Revenue

    Patreon + Airbnb yurt. Both separate from main funnel.

System Map

Instagram → meganrising.com → [write me / DM] → manual booking · Yurt: Airbnb · Blood Bone: Patreon. Three revenue streams, no unified funnel.

Phase 3 · Psychological Load

Why this feels heavy — and how to lighten it

External Problem

Every retreat inquiry becomes a DM requiring Megan's response.

Without FAQ + form, every visitor becomes a logistics conversation.

Values Alignment

Brand is freedom. Infrastructure should reflect that.

FAQ, forms, email list = infrastructure of freedom. They give Megan her time back.

The Transition Moment

Moving from club income to meadow income. Spring is proof-of-concept.

Beltane ($3k) = weekend at club. Form live by May = transition succeeds.

Phase 4 · Constraint Diagnosis

The one thing holding everything back

One Named Problem

Two pages are in the main nav and returning 404. Work with Me. 2026 Retreat Calendar. Every visitor who tries to book anything hits a dead end. Fix these first. Everything else is downstream.

Phase 5 · After State

What stable looks like once the infrastructure is in place

How Beltane Feels

Form live. Ten spots fill. $3k comes in. Megan does ceremony, not logistics.

How DMs Feel

Ask about Beltane. Megan: "See FAQ." Register. One sentence, not ten minutes.

How Spring Ends

Testimonials live. Photos on site. Next year's list fills on word-of-mouth before announcement.

How the Business Runs

Astrology bookings automated. Patreon featured. Yurt linked. All three streams visible and actionable.

Phase 6 · System Design Principles

Five rules for how this gets built

  1. Infrastructure before marketing.

    No SEO push, no campaigns until booking works. Build the door first.

  2. Let the FAQ do the customer service.

    Every DM question goes into FAQ. Megan's DMs become relationships.

  3. The event is the testimonial engine.

    Beltane = revenue + content. Every attendee is a word-of-mouth node.

  4. Connect the three revenue streams on the page.

    Yurt guest sees retreats. Patreon member sees Beltane. Site connects all three.

  5. Minimum viable tech, maximum clarity.

    Tally + Squarespace + Patreon + Airbnb. Make what exists work together.
