What I see from the outside, looking in

I help women remember who they are beneath the conditioning — through the wisdom of the stars, the intelligence of the body, and the medicine of sacred space.

That line, from meganrising.com, is already the whole brand. The stars. The body. The place. Three pillars in one sentence. What Megan has built is rarer than she may realize: she's not a practitioner who rents a space — she is the space. Twenty-seven acres in Floyd, Virginia. Off-grid. Real. Most spiritual practitioners sell transformation via Zoom. Megan's selling a portal you can actually walk through.

The site just launched, which means this isn't an audit of what went wrong — it's a map of what to build next, while the foundation is still warm. The brand identity is already strong. The gaps are structural and tactical, not identity-level. That's the best possible starting point.

Seven moves, in order of impact

  1. Fix the 404s — Work with Me and 2026 Retreat Calendar

    Both are in the main nav. Both are dead links. These are the two highest-converting pages on the site and neither resolves. Until they do, the navigation is actively sending visitors nowhere. Everything else waits on this.

  2. Get Tally registration forms live inside the Retreat Calendar page

    Beltane is May 2. $3,000 gross at 10 people. The calendar page needs to exist AND have a working form inside it. One without the other doesn't convert.

  3. Get testimonials live within 30 days

    The collection process is underway — handwritten testimonials + past guests via form. For a brand-new URL with no SEO authority, social proof is the conversion mechanism. A single powerful testimonial visible on the homepage does more work than any copy.

  4. Add pricing to astrology readings

    The offerings page lists sessions by length but no numbers. Price-curious visitors — the majority — need a figure to commit to or rule out. Showing pricing pre-qualifies leads, reduces back-and-forth, and signals confidence in the work's value.

  5. Build the Meadow FAQ as its own page section

    Bathrooms. Showers. Power. Pets. Alcohol. Packing. These questions are going to come in by DM until there's a page that answers them. The FAQ is also consent infrastructure — Megan's values (LGBTQ+/POC affirming, sex work positive, nudity may occur) need to be stated clearly so the right people feel welcomed and the wrong ones self-select out.

  6. Create a lead magnet and email capture

    A freebie builds the list between purchases — and Megan's audience wants to be in her world before they're ready to book. Options: a natal chart primer, a "what to bring to The Meadow" packing ritual guide, a Beltane intentions worksheet. Something that delivers value and captures email.

  7. Move the footer CTA up to the homepage hero

    "The cosmos has a plan. Your body has wisdom. Your soul has been waiting." is the best copy on the site and it's in the footer. Bring this three-part structure into the homepage — it maps perfectly to astrology / embodied practice / The Meadow and gives visitors an immediate orientation to all three pillars.

Phase 6 · System Design Principles

Five rules for how this gets built

  1. Infrastructure before marketing.

    No SEO push, no email campaigns, no Pinterest strategy until there is a way to book on the site. Sending traffic to a page with no conversion mechanism is wasted energy. Build the door first.

  2. Let the FAQ do the customer service.

    Every question Megan answers by DM is a signal. Each one goes into the FAQ. The goal is: zero repeated questions. The site answers them first. Megan's DMs become relationships, not logistics.

  3. The event is the testimonial engine.

    Beltane is not just revenue. It's content. Kristen's photos. Handwritten testimonials. Stories women tell their friends. Every person who attends is a word-of-mouth node. Make it beautiful enough to talk about. Make it easy to capture.

  4. Connect the three revenue streams on the page.

    A yurt guest should see the retreat calendar. A Patreon member should know when Beltane is. An astrology client should know about Blood Bone & Honey. The site connects dots the audience can't connect themselves.

  5. Minimum viable tech, maximum clarity.

    Tally for forms. Existing Squarespace/Canva site for pages. Patreon for membership. Airbnb for yurt. Don't migrate platforms — make what exists work together. Simplicity first, always.
