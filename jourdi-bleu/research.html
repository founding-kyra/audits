Brand Architecture Audit

What I see from the outside, looking in

When I started putting the breathwork and the coaching together, my business started working. The breathwork made my business work.

This single line from our discovery conversation is the whole audit. What Jourdi has built — a feminine + masculine integration of somatic breathwork and life coaching — produces results that talk-based approaches alone simply cannot reach. She knows it. Her clients feel it. The website doesn't say it yet.

Right now, jourdibleu.com leads with a tagline — 'Coaching for your most radiant self' — that could belong to any life coach anywhere. The actual client is a seeker who has already seen some light and knows there's more. There's a gap between who Jourdi is becoming and what the brand currently reflects. This audit maps that gap.

What jourdibleu.com says today

The site is a multi-page Showit build with dedicated pages for breathwork, speaking, events, and the home entry point. Copy quality varies significantly by page — and that variation is important data.

Homepage Tagline

'Coaching for your most radiant self'

Generic wellness positioning. Doesn't reflect the breathwork methodology, the somatic depth, or Jourdi's distinct voice. Notably weaker than what exists on the inner pages.

Breathwork Page — Strong

'Your breath is a doorway to your Upstairs Self'

'Upstairs Self' is a distinctive brand term that names the destination — better than "higher self," more embodied, more Jourdi. This page is substantially stronger than the homepage and isn't being leveraged.

Speaking Page — Strong

'telling true stories… that remind them who the F*CK they are'

This is the real Jourdi voice. Raw, purposeful, confident. The speaking page has the best copy on the entire site — and most visitors never find it.

Navigation

Welcome Home · Breathwork · Work With Me · Speaking · Events · Gifts

Solid structure. The problem is the homepage doesn't give enough signal about what kind of work this is, so many visitors won't click deeper to find what makes it different.

Seven moves, in order of impact

  1. Evolve the tagline beyond 'Sober. Happy. Free.'

    True and earned, but it anchors the brand in a chapter Jourdi has moved through. The next tagline calls in the seeker who knows there's more. The sobriety story becomes credibility, not positioning.

  2. Name and own the integrated methodology

    Breathwork + coaching as one named thing. She didn't combine two services — she built something that works because of the integration. That needs a name and a prominent home.

  3. Rewrite the homepage to speak directly to the seeker

    Lead with the core tension: You know you're capable of more. You've done the work. And still something isn't moving. This is the reader. This is the moment she's in when she finds Jourdi.

  4. Bring 'Upstairs Self' to the front door

    'Upstairs' already appears on the breathwork and speaking pages. It's specific, proprietary, and immediately tells a visitor what the work is for. It belongs on the homepage.

  5. Put group sessions front and center as the entry point

    Build the teaser-to-container funnel Jourdi already described. Group breathwork is the first handshake. Make it visible, accessible, and clearly framed as the beginning of something.

  6. Integrate credentials into the main brand bio

    950 hours of training. Criminology background. Life Coaching Diploma. This lives only on the speaking page — it needs to be visible in the main About section.

  7. Build transformation stories with specificity

    The outcomes are extraordinary. These need to be on the site — specific, felt, with permission. Testimonials aren't fluff here. They're proof the methodology works.

Where to focus first

Priority 01 — Quick Win

Meta titles + descriptions

Every page needs a unique, keyword-rich meta title (50–60 chars) and meta description (150–160 chars). These don't require redesign — they're set in the Showit CMS. This alone improves click-through rate immediately.

Priority 02 — 30 Days

Blog — First 3 posts

Target: "What is rebirthing breathwork," "Breathwork vs. therapy: what's the difference," "What to expect in your first breathwork session." All high-intent informational searches with low competition.

Priority 03 — 60 Days

Add Group Sessions page

A dedicated page for group breathwork sessions serves dual purpose: it captures 'group breathwork [city]' searches AND creates the entry-point funnel to 1:1 work. Currently this offer has no home of its own.

Priority 04 — Ongoing

Internal linking strategy

Link from blog posts → Breathwork page → Work With Me. Create a deliberate path from informational content to conversion. Every post should have one internal link and one CTA.

Offer Map · Architecture + Pricing

The money structure needs to match the transformation arc.

Jourdi's current offer suite is real and broad — but it doesn't tell a story. A visitor can't feel where to start, what leads to what, or what the full investment in working with her looks like. A clear tiered structure solves all three problems at once.
